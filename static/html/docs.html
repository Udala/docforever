What are the practical uses of Docforever?

Docforever proves that a document existed at the time of registration or earlier. This feature may be useful to achieve several goals.

  • Protect your idea from patents: Novelty is a patentability requirement. A patent becomes void if you prove that the content of a patent is prior-art (defensive publication).
  • Protect your creations from demands of plagiarism.
  • Add a time-dimension to your personal memories.
  • Crytographically bind documents or images in a single invulnerable fingerprint
  • Switch the burden of proof in some legal affairs.

What do I need to prove the existence of my document?

It is your responsibility to keep the unmodified document and the Bitcoin-Merkle file for your document.

How do I download the Bitcoin-Merkle file?

The Bitcoin-Merkle file for your document will only be available after 23:59:59 UTC of the same day of registration.

If you register your document at 00:01:00 UTC you will need to wait 24 hours. On the other hand, if you register your document at 23:59:00 UTC, the Bitcoin-Merkle file will be available for download in just a few seconds.

The Bitcoin-Merkle files are only stored on our servers for seven days after publication on the Blockchain.

I registered my document yesterday. I want to download my Bitcoin-Merkle file. How do I proceed?

Just go to the Docforever homepage and "upload" your document. Only the hash of your document will be uploaded to our servers. We verify that the Merkle root that represents the tree that contains the fingerprint of your document has already been broadcast to the Bitcoin network. If that is the case, you will immediately be able to download your Bitcoin-Merkle file. If not, we will inform you how long you still need to wait until it is ready.

How can Docforever be free?

Registering thousands of hashes directly on the blockchain would be prohibitively expensive for a user. Moreover, it would lead to unnecessary traffic on the network. Docforever stores all those hashes offline throughout the day and only registers the global Merkle root once, at the end of the day, on the Bitcoin blockchain.

Do you give any guarantee that your service works correctly?

No. We do our best to provide an accurate service, but we can not guarantee that Docforever works flawlessly.

When you come back to the website for your Bitcoin-Merkle file, we also provide some scripts that prove the binding between the transaction identifier on the Bitcoin blockchain and your document via the Bitcoin-Merkle file. Be diligent and check for yourself.

Docforever is open source. You can analyze the code or fork it if you feel like it.
